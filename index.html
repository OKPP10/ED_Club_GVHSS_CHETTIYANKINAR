INAUGURATION

We are excited to announce the launch of the Entrepreneurship Development Club at GVHSS Chettiyanknar! This is the first time our school is establishing such a club, aimed at igniting and fostering entrepreneurial spirit among students. The club will serve as a platform for students to explore the fundamentals of entrepreneurship, develop critical skills like leadership, problem-solving, and creativity, and learn the importance of innovation in today's world. Through various activities, workshops, and mentorship sessions, the Entrepreneurship Development Club will empower students to transform ideas into actionable projects and even real-world ventures. Join us for the inauguration ceremony on November 6th to celebrate this milestone and to begin an inspiring journey of learning, growth, and enterprise!

OBJECTIVE

  • To inculcate both the technical skill and managerial skill in budding entrepreneurs
  • Feasibility, prototype, and knowledge of the institutional research can be transferred to Entrepreneurship students through the scheme
  • Opportunities abound for graduate students to develop independent projects and launch their own careers as researchers while working alongside faculty and assisting in their research.
  • Field support from the department, research support from the research institutions and curriculum support from the university can be converged for young entrepreneurs to commercialise the potential projects
  • Knowledge has become the new premium fuel for economic growth in the 21st century. Knowledge fuels new ideas and innovations to boost productivity and to create new products, new firms, new jobs, and new wealth. Some analysts estimate that knowledge-based activity accounts for half of the gross domestic product in Western industrialized countries.
  • Serve as corporate headquarters for knowledge-based companies / organizations
  • Facilitate applied or fundamental research and development of technologies
  • Facilitate testing and product prototyping
  • Catalyze development and delivery of information technologies, biotechnologies, other scientific areas including service-based businesse
  • Incubate knowledge-based business start-ups
  • Some of the expected gains are more interdisciplinary research and new innovations, lower transaction cost, more efficient use of science and technology, more discoveries relevant to innovation, more knowledge based businesses and economic growth.
